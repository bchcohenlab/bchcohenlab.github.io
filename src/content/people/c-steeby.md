Clara graduated from the University of Michigan in 2020 with a bachelor’s degree in Neuroscience. Her previous research experience involves studying attention and distraction in ADHD using eye tracking and investigating GABA function and negative affect in psychosis spectrum disorders using fMRI and MRS. She has developed interests in cognition, psychosis, and neuroimaging techniques such as fMRI. In the future, she plans to earn a PhD in clinical psychology to pursue research and clinical practice related to psychosis-spectrum disorders. In her free time, Clara enjoys reading fiction, baking, and has recently started silk screen printing.
